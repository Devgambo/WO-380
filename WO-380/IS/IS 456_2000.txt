# IS 456:2000 - Plain and Reinforced Concrete Code of Practice: Comprehensive Analysis

## **Document Overview**

**IS 456:2000** is the fundamental Indian Standard for **Plain and Reinforced Concrete - Code of Practice (Fourth Revision)**. This is arguably the most important structural engineering standard in India, governing the design, construction, and quality control of concrete structures across the country.

### **Key Document Details:**
- **Standard Number:** IS 456:2000
- **Title:** Plain and Reinforced Concrete - Code of Practice
- **Revision:** Fourth Revision (Reaffirmed 2005)
- **Publication:** July 2000, Tenth Reprint April 2007
- **Scope:** General structural use of plain and reinforced concrete
- **Authority:** Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS)

***

## **SECTION 1: GENERAL PROVISIONS**

### **1. Scope and Applicability**
- **Primary Application:** General structural use of plain and reinforced concrete
- **Plain Concrete Structures:** Those where reinforcement is ignored for strength determination
- **Exclusions:** Special structures like shells, folded plates, arches, bridges, chimneys, blast-resistant structures, hydraulic structures, liquid retaining structures, and earthquake-resistant structures (covered in respective standards)

### **2. Referenced Standards**
The code references **77 Indian Standards** covering materials, testing, loads, and construction practices, ensuring comprehensive integration with the Indian standards ecosystem.

### **3. Terminology and Symbols**
- **Standardized Definitions:** Based on IS 4845 and IS 6461
- **Comprehensive Symbol System:** 50+ symbols covering dimensions, forces, material properties, and design parameters
- **Key Symbols Include:**
  - **A:** Area
  - **fck:** Characteristic compressive strength of concrete
  - **fy:** Characteristic strength of steel
  - **M:** Bending moment
  - **V:** Shear force

## **SECTION 2: MATERIALS, WORKMANSHIP, INSPECTION AND TESTING**

### **5. Materials Specifications**

#### **5.1 Cement Requirements**
**Approved Cement Types:**
1. **33 Grade OPC** (IS 269)
2. **43 Grade OPC** (IS 8112)
3. **53 Grade OPC** (IS 12269)
4. **Rapid Hardening Portland Cement** (IS 8041)
5. **Portland Slag Cement** (IS 455)
6. **Portland Pozzolana Cement** - Fly ash based (IS 1489 Part 1)
7. **Portland Pozzolana Cement** - Calcined clay based (IS 1489 Part 2)
8. **Hydrophobic Cement** (IS 8043)
9. **Low Heat Portland Cement** (IS 12600)
10. **Sulphate Resisting Portland Cement** (IS 12330)

#### **5.2 Mineral Admixtures**
- **Fly Ash:** Grade 1 of IS 3812, uniform blending required
- **Silica Fume:** 5-10% of cement content, very fine non-crystalline silicon dioxide
- **Rice Husk Ash:** Subject to performance evaluation
- **Metakaoline:** Fineness 700-900 m²/kg
- **Ground Granulated Blast Furnace Slag:** Per IS 12089

#### **5.3 Aggregates**
- **Primary Standard:** IS 383 compliance mandatory
- **Natural Aggregates:** Preferred where possible
- **Alternative Aggregates:** Slag, crushed overburnt brick/tile (≤0.5% sulphates, ≤10% water absorption)
- **Heavy/Light Weight Aggregates:** Subject to engineering approval

**Size Requirements:**
- **Maximum Size:** ≤1/4 minimum member thickness
- **Standard Sizes:** 20mm suitable for most work, 10mm for thin sections
- **Plums:** Up to 160mm, maximum 20% by volume in plain concrete

#### **5.4 Water Quality**
**Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Potable Water:** Generally acceptable
- **pH Value:** ≥6.0
- **Chemical Limits:**
  - Organic matter: ≤200 mg/l
  - Inorganic matter: ≤3000 mg/l
  - Sulphates (as SO₃): ≤400 mg/l
  - Chlorides: ≤2000 mg/l (plain), ≤500 mg/l (reinforced)
  - Suspended matter: ≤2000 mg/l

**Performance Tests:**
- **Strength Test:** ≥90% of distilled water strength
- **Setting Time:** Within ±30 minutes of control

#### **5.5 Admixtures**
- **Standard:** IS 9103 compliance
- **Types:** Plasticizers, superplasticizers, retarders
- **Dosage Limits:**
  - Retarders: ≤0.5% by weight of cement
  - Plasticizers: ≤1.0% by weight of cement
  - Superplasticizers: ≤2.0% by weight of cement

#### **5.6 Reinforcement**
**Approved Types:**
1. **Mild/Medium Tensile Steel:** IS 432 (Part 1)
2. **High Strength Deformed Bars:** IS 1786
3. **Hard-drawn Steel Wire Fabric:** IS 1566
4. **Structural Steel:** Grade A of IS 2062

**Key Requirements:**
- **Modulus of Elasticity:** 200 kN/mm²
- **Surface Condition:** Free from loose mill scales, rust, paint, oil, mud
- **Special Coatings:** May be required for exceptional cases

### **6. Concrete Grades and Properties**

#### **6.1 Concrete Grades**
**Standard Designation System:**
- **M10 to M80:** Where number indicates characteristic compressive strength in N/mm²
- **Minimum Grades:**
  - Plain concrete: As per Table 5
  - Reinforced concrete: M20 minimum recommended

#### **6.2 Concrete Properties**
**Strength Development:**
- **Design Basis:** 28-day characteristic strength
- **Age Factor:** Strength gain beyond 28 days considered for grades M30 and above

**Tensile Strength:**
- **Flexural Strength:** fcr = 0.7√fck N/mm²

**Elastic Properties:**
- **Modulus of Elasticity:** Ec = 5000√fck N/mm²
- **Actual Values:** May vary ±20% from calculated values

**Time-Dependent Properties:**
- **Shrinkage Strain:** 0.0003 (approximate total)
- **Creep Coefficients:** 2.2 (7 days), 1.6 (28 days), 1.1 (1 year)
- **Thermal Expansion:** 0.6×10⁻⁵ to 1.3×10⁻⁵ per °C

### **7. Workability Requirements**

**Slump Categories:**
- **Very Low:** Special control needed (compacting factor 0.75-0.80)
- **Low:** 25-75mm (mass concrete, lightly reinforced sections)
- **Medium:** 50-100mm (heavily reinforced sections)
- **High:** 75-100mm (pumped concrete)
- **Very High:** 100-150mm (tremie concrete, in-situ piling)

### **8. Durability Requirements**

#### **8.2 Environmental Exposure Classification**
**Five Exposure Categories:**
1. **Mild:** Protected from weather/aggressive conditions
2. **Moderate:** Sheltered from severe rain/freezing, underwater, buried in non-aggressive soil
3. **Severe:** Exposed to severe rain, wetting/drying cycles, seawater immersion
4. **Very Severe:** Seawater spray, corrosive fumes, severe freezing, aggressive soil/groundwater
5. **Extreme:** Tidal zone, direct contact with aggressive chemicals

#### **8.2.4 Concrete Mix Requirements**

**Minimum Cement Content and Maximum W/C Ratio:**

| Exposure | Plain Concrete | | Reinforced Concrete | |
|----------|----------------|--|---------------------|--|
| | Min Cement (kg/m³) | Max W/C | Min Cement (kg/m³) | Max W/C |
| Mild | 220 | 0.60 | 300 | 0.55 |
| Moderate | 240 | 0.60 | 300 | 0.50 |
| Severe | 250 | 0.50 | 320 | 0.45 |
| Very Severe | 260 | 0.45 | 340 | 0.45 |
| Extreme | 280 | 0.40 | 360 | 0.40 |

**Minimum Concrete Grades:**

| Exposure | Plain Concrete | Reinforced Concrete |
|----------|----------------|---------------------|
| Mild | - | M20 |
| Moderate | M15 | M25 |
| Severe | M20 | M30 |
| Very Severe | M20 | M35 |
| Extreme | M25 | M40 |

#### **8.2.5 Chemical Limits**
**Chloride Content Limits (% by mass of concrete):**
- **Prestressed/Steam Cured:** ≤0.4%
- **Reinforced Concrete:** ≤0.6%
- **Plain Concrete:** ≤1.0%

**Sulphate Content:** ≤4% by mass of cement (total water-soluble)

**Alkali-Aggregate Reaction Prevention:**
- Use non-reactive aggregates
- Low alkali cement (≤0.6% Na₂O equivalent)
- Minimum 20% fly ash or 50% slag content
- Control moisture exposure

### **9. Concrete Mix Proportioning**

#### **9.2 Design Mix Concrete**
**Target Mean Strength:**
- **Formula:** Target = fck + 1.65 × Standard Deviation
- **Standard Deviation:** Based on 30+ test results or assumed values (Table 8)

**Assumed Standard Deviations:**
- **M10-M20:** 3.5-4.0 N/mm²
- **M25-M50:** 4.0-5.0 N/mm²

#### **9.3 Nominal Mix Concrete**
**Permitted for:** M20 and lower grades
**Mix Proportions (Cement:Fine:Coarse Aggregate):**
- **M5:** 1:5:10
- **M7.5:** 1:4:8
- **M10:** 1:3:6
- **M15:** 1:2:4
- **M20:** 1:1.5:3

### **10-17. Production, Quality Control, and Testing**

#### **10.1 Quality Assurance**
**Key Elements:**
- Technical and organizational measures
- Input/output quality audit
- Comprehensive documentation system
- Statistical quality control charts

#### **10.2 Batching**
**Accuracy Requirements:**
- **Cement:** ±2%
- **Aggregates:** ±3%
- **Water/Admixtures:** ±3%
- **Mass Batching:** Preferred over volume batching

#### **15. Sampling and Testing**
**Frequency:**
- **1-15 m³:** 1 sample
- **16-30 m³:** 2 samples  
- **31-50 m³:** 3 samples
- **51+ m³:** 4 samples + 1 per additional 50 m³

**Test Specimens:** 3 cubes per sample for 28-day testing

#### **16. Acceptance Criteria**
**Compliance Requirements:**
1. **Mean of 4 consecutive results:** ≥ fck + 0.825 × σ (or fck + 3-4 N/mm²)
2. **Individual result:** ≥ fck - 3-4 N/mm²

## **SECTION 3: GENERAL DESIGN CONSIDERATIONS**

### **18. Design Philosophy**
**Limit State Method:** Primary design approach
**Working Stress Method:** Alternative approach (Annex B)
**Experimental Basis:** Permitted with adequate validation

### **19. Loads and Forces**
**Load Types:**
- **Dead Loads:** Unit weights per IS 875 (Part 1)
- **Imposed Loads:** Per IS 875 (Part 2)
- **Wind Loads:** Per IS 875 (Part 3)
- **Snow Loads:** Per IS 875 (Part 4)
- **Earthquake Forces:** Per IS 1893
- **Special Effects:** Shrinkage, creep, temperature, foundation movement

**Load Combinations:** Per IS 875 (Part 5)

### **20. Structural Stability**
**Overturning Stability:**
- **Safety Factor:** 1.4 minimum
- **Dead Load Factor:** 0.9 when providing restoring moment
- **Imposed Load Factor:** 1.4 for overturning, ignored for restoring

**Sliding Resistance:**
- **Safety Factor:** 1.4 minimum
- **Lateral Sway:** ≤H/500 under wind loads

### **21. Fire Resistance**
**Requirements:** Per IS 1641 and IS 1642
**Minimum Cover and Dimensions:** Specified in Table 16A and Figure 1
**Fire Resistance Periods:** 0.5 to 4 hours classification

### **22. Structural Analysis**
**Methods:**
- **Linear Elastic Theory:** Primary method
- **Simplified Analysis:** For frames and continuous beams
- **Moment Redistribution:** Up to 30% permitted with conditions

#### **22.2 Effective Span**
**Simply Supported:** Clear span + effective depth (or center-to-center, whichever less)
**Continuous:** Varies based on support width and end conditions
**Cantilever:** Length to face of support + half effective depth

### **23. Beams**
#### **23.1 T-Beams and L-Beams**
**Effective Flange Width:**
- **T-beams:** beff = lo/12 + bw + 6Df
- **L-beams:** beff = lo/12 + bw + 3Df
- **Maximum:** Actual flange width or web + half adjacent clear distances

#### **23.2 Deflection Control**
**Limits:**
- **Total Deflection:** ≤span/250
- **Post-construction:** ≤span/350 or 20mm (whichever less)

**Span-to-Depth Ratios (Basic Values):**
- **Cantilever:** 7
- **Simply Supported:** 20  
- **Continuous:** 26

**Modifications for:**
- **Spans >10m:** Multiply by 10/span
- **Tension Reinforcement:** Per Figure 4
- **Compression Reinforcement:** Per Figure 5
- **Flanged Beams:** Per Figure 6

### **24. Slabs**
#### **24.4 Two-Way Slabs**
**Design Methods:**
- **Elastic Methods:** Grashof's, Westergaard's theory
- **Yield Line Theory:** Johansen's method
- **Coefficient Method:** Annex D

#### **24.3.2 Concentrated Loads**
**Effective Width:** Per complex formulae considering load position and slab geometry

### **25. Compression Members**
#### **25.1 Classifications**
**Column/Strut:** Effective length > 3 × least lateral dimension
**Short Column:** lex/D < 12 and ley/b < 12
**Slender Column:** Otherwise

#### **25.2 Effective Length**
**Determination:** Per Annex E or simplified Table 28
**Typical Values:**
- **Fixed-Fixed:** 0.50l to 0.65l
- **Pinned-Pinned:** 1.00l
- **Fixed-Free:** 2.00l

#### **25.4 Minimum Eccentricity**
**Formula:** emin = l/500 + D/30 ≥ 20mm
**Biaxial Bending:** Consider one axis at a time

### **26. Reinforcement Detailing**

#### **26.2 Development Length**
**Formula:** Ld = φσs/(4τbd)
**Design Bond Stress (Plain Bars):**
- **M20:** 1.2 N/mm²
- **M25:** 1.4 N/mm²  
- **M30:** 1.5 N/mm²
- **M35:** 1.7 N/mm²
- **M40+:** 1.9 N/mm²
**Deformed Bars:** 60% increase

#### **26.2.2 Anchorage**
**Standard Hook:** 16φ anchorage value
**Bend Value:** 4φ per 45° (maximum 16φ)

#### **26.3 Spacing Requirements**
**Minimum Spacing:**
- **Horizontal:** Greater of bar diameter, larger bar diameter, or aggregate size + 5mm
- **Vertical:** 15mm, 2/3 aggregate size, or bar diameter (whichever greater)

**Maximum Spacing (Tension Bars):**
- **Beams:** Per Table 15 (varies with steel grade and redistribution)
- **Slabs:** 3d or 300mm (main), 5d or 450mm (distribution)

#### **26.4 Concrete Cover**
**Durability Requirements:**
- **Mild:** 20mm
- **Moderate:** 30mm  
- **Severe:** 45mm
- **Very Severe:** 50mm
- **Extreme:** 75mm

**Fire Resistance Requirements:** Per Table 16A (20-70mm based on fire rating)

**Special Cases:**
- **Columns:** Minimum 40mm or bar diameter
- **Footings:** Minimum 50mm

#### **26.5 Minimum Reinforcement**

**Beams:**
- **Tension:** Ast,min = 0.85bd/fy
- **Maximum:** 4% of gross area
- **Compression:** Maximum 4% of gross area

**Slabs:**
- **Minimum:** 0.15% for mild steel, 0.12% for high-strength steel
- **Maximum Bar Diameter:** D/8

**Columns:**
- **Longitudinal:** 0.8% to 6% of gross area
- **Minimum Bars:** 4 (rectangular), 6 (circular)
- **Bar Diameter:** ≥12mm
- **Spacing:** ≤300mm on periphery

**Walls:**
- **Vertical:** 0.0012 (deformed), 0.0015 (others)
- **Horizontal:** 0.0020 (deformed), 0.0025 (others)
- **Spacing:** ≤3t or 450mm

## **SECTION 4: SPECIAL DESIGN REQUIREMENTS**

### **28. Concrete Corbels**
**Definition:** Short cantilever with av < d and depth at contact ≥ 0.5d at root
**Design Approach:** Strut-and-tie system
**Horizontal Force:** ≥0.5 vertical design load

### **29. Deep Beams**
**Definition:** l/D < 2.0 (simply supported), l/D < 2.5 (continuous)
**Lever Arm:** Special provisions for reduced effective depth
**Reinforcement:** Special distribution requirements for positive and negative moments

### **30. Ribbed/Hollow Block Slabs**
**Types:**
- Removable forms
- Permanent precast blocks  
- Continuous voided construction

**Rib Requirements:**
- **Minimum Width:** 65mm
- **Maximum Spacing:** 1.5m centers
- **Depth Limitation:** ≤4 × width

### **31. Flat Slabs**
**Definition:** Slab supported directly on columns with/without drops/capitals

#### **31.2 Proportioning**
**Thickness:** Per span-to-depth ratios (multiply by 0.9 if no drops meeting requirements)
**Minimum Thickness:** 125mm

**Drops (if provided):**
- **Size:** ≥1/3 panel dimension each direction
- **Exterior Panels:** Half width of interior drops

#### **31.3 Analysis Methods**
1. **Direct Design Method** (31.4)
2. **Equivalent Frame Method** (31.5)

**Direct Design Limitations:**
- ≥3 continuous spans each direction
- Rectangular panels (long/short ≤ 2.0)  
- Column offset ≤10% of span
- Span variation ≤1/3 of longer span
- Live load ≤3 × dead load

#### **31.4 Direct Design Method**
**Total Design Moment:** Mo = WlnLx²/8
**Distribution:**
- **Interior Span:** Negative 0.65, Positive 0.35
- **End Span:** Variable based on fixity ratio αc

#### **31.5 Equivalent Frame Method**
**Column Strip:** 0.25lx on each side of column centerline
**Middle Strip:** Remainder between column strips

**Moment Distribution:**
- **Interior Support (Column Strip):** 75% of negative moment
- **Exterior Support (Column Strip):** 100% of negative moment  
- **Mid-span (Column Strip):** 60% of positive moment

#### **31.6 Shear in Flat Slabs**
**Critical Section:** d/2 from column/capital face
**Shear Stress:** V/(bod) + moment transfer effects
**Permissible Stress:** ks·tc where ks = (0.5 + βc) ≤ 1.0, tc = 0.25√fck

### **32. Walls**
**Minimum Thickness:** 100mm

#### **32.2 Empirical Design (Braced Walls)**
**Maximum H/t Ratio:** 30
**Effective Height:** Depends on end restraint (0.75l to 1.0l)
**Design Strength:** Pu = 0.3(t - 1.2e - 2ea)fck

#### **32.4 Horizontal Shear Design**
**Critical Section:** 0.5Lw or 0.5Hw from base (whichever less)
**Shear Strength:** Variable with Hw/Lw ratio
**Maximum Stress:** 0.17fck (limit state), 0.12fck (working stress)

### **33. Stairs**
**Effective Span:** Depends on support conditions and landing arrangements
**Depth:** Minimum thickness perpendicular to soffit

### **34. Footings**
**Minimum Thickness:** 150mm (on soil), 300mm (on piles)

#### **34.2 Critical Sections**
**Moment:** At column face (concrete), midway for masonry
**Shear:** 
- **Wide Beam Action:** d from column face
- **Punching Shear:** Per flat slab provisions

**Load Transfer:** Bearing stress ≤ permissible × √(A1/A2) ≤ 2.0 × permissible

## **SECTION 5: STRUCTURAL DESIGN (LIMIT STATE METHOD)**

### **35. Safety and Serviceability Requirements**

#### **35.2 Limit State of Collapse**
**Failure Modes:** Bending, shear, torsion, axial loads, buckling, overturning

#### **35.3 Serviceability Limits**
**Deflection:** Per Section 23.2
**Cracking:** Surface crack widths:
- **Normal Exposure:** ≤0.3mm
- **Corrosive Environment:** ≤0.2mm  
- **Severe Environment:** ≤0.1mm

### **36. Partial Safety Factors**

#### **36.4.1 Load Factors (γf)**
**Limit State of Collapse:**
- **DL + IL:** 1.5 DL + 1.5 IL
- **DL + WL:** 1.5 DL + 1.5 WL (or 0.9 DL + 1.5 WL)
- **DL + IL + WL:** 1.2 DL + 1.2 IL + 1.2 WL

**Serviceability:** All loads taken as 1.0

#### **36.4.2 Material Factors (γm)**
- **Concrete:** 1.5
- **Steel:** 1.15

### **37. Analysis and Moment Redistribution**
**Redistribution Limits:**
- **Maximum Reduction:** 30% of elastic moment
- **Support Moment:** ≥70% of elastic moment
- **Neutral Axis Limit:** xu/d ≤ (0.6 - δM/100) where δM = % moment reduction

### **38. Flexural Design**

#### **38.1 Assumptions**
- **Strain Compatibility:** Plane sections remain plane
- **Maximum Concrete Strain:** 0.0035
- **Stress-Strain:** Rectangle, trapezoid, parabola, or other approved curves
- **Design Strength:** 0.67fck with γm = 1.5
- **Tensile Strength:** Ignored
- **Minimum Steel Strain:** fy/(1.15Es) + 0.002

**Limiting xu/d Ratios:**
- **Fe 250:** 0.53
- **Fe 415:** 0.48  
- **Fe 500:** 0.46

### **39. Compression Design**

#### **39.3 Short Axially Loaded Members**
**Design Formula:** Pu = 0.4fck·Ac + 0.67fy·Asc

#### **39.4 Helical Reinforcement**
**Strength Increase:** 1.05 times tied column strength
**Volumetric Ratio:** ≥0.36(Ag/Ac - 1)fck/fy

#### **39.5-39.6 Combined Loading**
**Uniaxial Bending:** Strain compatibility analysis
**Biaxial Bending:** [Mux1/Mux1]^αn + [Muy1/Muy1]^αn ≤ 1.0

#### **39.7 Slender Columns**
**Additional Moments:**
- **Major Axis:** Max = Pu·D(lex/D)²/2000
- **Minor Axis:** May = Pu·b(ley/b)²/2000

### **40. Shear Design**

#### **40.1 Nominal Shear Stress**
**Formula:** τv = Vu/(b·d)

#### **40.2 Design Shear Strength**
**Concrete Contribution:** Per Table 19 (varies with reinforcement percentage and concrete grade)
**Typical Values:** 0.28-1.01 N/mm² based on pt% and grade

**Axial Compression Effect:** Multiply by (1 + Pu/(6fck·Ag)) ≤ 1.5

#### **40.3-40.4 Shear Reinforcement**
**Minimum:** Per 26.5.1.6 when τv < τc
**Design:** Vus = 0.87·fy·Asv·d/sv

**Forms:**
- **Vertical stirrups:** Most common
- **Inclined stirrups:** 45° minimum
- **Bent-up bars:** ≤50% of total shear reinforcement

#### **40.5 Enhanced Shear Near Supports**
**Condition:** Section within 2d of support face
**Enhancement:** τc increased to 2d·τc/av
**Maximum:** Still limited by τc,max values

### **41. Torsion Design**

#### **41.3 Equivalent Shear**
**Formula:** Ve = Vu + 1.6Tu/b
**Limit:** τve ≤ τc,max

#### **41.4 Torsion Reinforcement**
**Longitudinal:** For equivalent moment Mt = Tu(1 + D/b)/1.7
**Transverse:** Two-legged closed stirrups with area per complex formula

### **42-43. Serviceability**
**Deflection:** Usually controlled by span-to-depth ratios
**Cracking:** Controlled by reinforcement spacing limits

1. **Project Assessment:** Exposure conditions, loading, importance
2. **Material Selection:** Concrete grade, cement type, reinforcement grade

5. **Detailed Design:** Flexure, shear, torsion, serviceability checks
6. **Detailing:** Reinforcement arrangement, covers, anchorages
7. **Quality Control:** Construction specifications, testing requirements

- **Residential Buildings:** M20-M25
- **Commercial Buildings:** M25-M30

- **Primary Choice:** Fe 415 (HYSD bars)

**IS 456:2000** represents a comprehensive and mature approach to concrete construction, balancing **safety, serviceability, economy, and durability**. Its strength lies in:

The standard has successfully established a **unified framework** for concrete construction in India, contributing significantly to infrastructure development and construction quality improvement. **For practitioners**, IS 456:2000 serves as an indispensable reference that combines **technical rigor with practical applicability**, making it the cornerstone of reinforced concrete design and construction in India.
